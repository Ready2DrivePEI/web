# How PEI Licensing Works

## Lesson 1: What a Driver's Licence Really Means

### Licence Basics
A driver's licence is more than just an ID. It’s a legal agreement that you have the skills to drive specific vehicles under safe conditions. In PEI, keeping that privilege depends on following your class rules and making sure your licence stays in good standing.

> **Info:** Think of your licence like a specialized tool that gives you privilege within specified limits

### When a Licence Is Not Valid
| Status | What It Means for Driving |
| --- | --- |
| Expired | The date has passed; you'll need to renew this before you can legally drive again. |
| Suspended | Driving privileges are temporarily paused. |
| Cancelled | Driving privileges have been removed. |
| Other invalid status | Any legal invalid status means it's not safe or legal for you to be behind the wheel. |

### Class Rules and Condition Rules
Your licence is tailored to you. If it lists conditions like needing glasses, those are there to help you drive safely. Following the rules attached to your specific class is the best way to protect your new privilege.

> **Warning:** It's easy to overlook, but please remember: the class number on your card dictates exactly what you're trained for. Remembering your conditions helps you stay compliant.

> **Info:** Tip: Before borrowing a friend's vehicle, double-check that your class allows for it. It only takes a second!

## Lesson 2: PEI Licence Classes (Class 1-9) Made Simple

### How the Class System Works
PEI uses classes to match your training with the vehicle's complexity. Each step up the ladder usually involves more weight or more passengers, which means a higher level of responsibility for you as a driver.

> **Info:** Don't worry about memorizing the whole list. The main goal is understanding that each class represents a different level of safety and skill.

### Beginner and Personal Classes
- Class 7: Instruction Driver's Permit (Your starting point)
- Class 5: Standard cars and light trucks
- Class 6: Motorcycles
- Class 8: Mopeds or motor-assisted pedal bicycles
- Class 9: Farm tractors

### Commercial and Higher-Risk Classes
- Class 1: Large truck tractor and trailer combinations
- Class 2: Large buses (over 24 passengers), including school buses
- Class 3: Heavy trucks over 14,000 kg
- Class 4: Taxis, ambulances, vans, or small buses

### At-a-Glance Class Table summary
| Class | Typical use | Core idea |

> **Note:** Most drivers find that mastering Class 7 and then Class 5 provides all the freedom they need

## Lesson 3: Your Beginner Pathway (GDL) From Day 1

### Where You Start
Receiving your Class 7 permit is an exciting first step. You're now part of the Graduated Driver Licensing (GDL) system. This program is designed to give you road experience in a controlled way that keeps you and everyone else safe while you're still learning. Details on how you obtain a Class 7 permit are covered in the next chapter.

> **Info:** The first stage of the GDL program focuses on building foundational driving skills. The rules are designed to support safe learning and skill development.

### Stage 1: Requirements, Restrictions, and Timing
| Category | The Goal of the Rule |
| --- | --- |
| Supervision:- | Ensures you have an experienced driver to guide you through tricky situations. |
| Supervisor Standard | Your supervisor must have held a valid licence for at least four years. |
| Vehicles Allowed | You can practice in passenger vehicles, motorcycles, and mopeds. |
| Required Decal | The yellow L decal helps other drivers be patient and give you the space you need. |
| Core Restrictions | Zero alcohol (BAC) and no handheld devices help you stay focused. |
| Night Restriction | No driving between 1:00 a.m. and 5:00 a.m. to avoid high-risk hours. |
| Road Test Timing | You're eligible after 365 days (or 275 days with an approved driver ed course). |
| Permit Validity | Your permit lasts two years, giving you plenty of time to feel ready for your test. |

### What Happens Next
Once you've completed your waiting period and feel confident in your skills, you can book your Class 5 road test. Success here is about showing you've developed the safe habits and maturity needed to drive on your own.

> **Warning:** Remember, the GDL program is about quality practice. Following the restrictions now helps ensure you're truly ready for the road later.

### A Quick Summary
- Class 7 is your learning phase.
- Always drive with your supervisor.
- Respect the safety restrictions; they are there for your protection.
- Focus on experience, and Class 5 will follow naturally.
