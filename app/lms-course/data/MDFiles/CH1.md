## M1 CH1 Lesson 1: What a Driver's Licence Really Means

### Learning outcome
By the end of this lesson, learners can explain a driver's licence as a legal privilege with conditions, not just an ID card.

### Page content
**Start here**  
A driver's licence is legal permission to drive, but only under specific conditions.

**Core idea**  
In PEI, your licence must be valid. It cannot be expired, suspended, cancelled, or otherwise invalid.  
Your licence only covers the class(es) of vehicle shown on it, and you must follow any restrictions printed on the licence.

**Why this matters**  
Driving is treated as a safety responsibility. Privileges are maintained through safe behavior and legal compliance.

**Example scenario**  
"I have a licence, so I can drive anything."  
Not correct. Your licence class determines what vehicles you are legally allowed to operate.

**Quick recap**
- A licence is a legal driving privilege.
- It only applies to approved class(es).
- Conditions/restrictions are enforceable.

## M1 CH1 Lesson 2: PEI Licence Classes (Class 1-9) Without the Confusion

### Learning outcome
By the end of this lesson, learners can identify the purpose of each PEI licence class and which classes matter most for beginner drivers.

### Page content
**Start here**  
PEI uses a classified licensing system. Different vehicle types require different training and legal privileges.

**Class map (beginner-first view)**
- `Class 7`: Instruction permit (beginner stage)
- `Class 5`: Standard passenger vehicle licence (most learners aim here first)
- `Class 6`: Motorcycles
- `Class 8`: Mopeds (pedal-assisted)
- `Class 9`: Farm tractors

**Commercial/large-vehicle classes (awareness)**
- `Class 1`: Truck-tractor/trailer combinations (not buses)
- `Class 2`: Buses carrying more than 24 passengers (including school buses)
- `Class 3`: Trucks over 14,000 kg and related combinations
- `Class 4`: Ambulance, taxi, and buses carrying 24 passengers or fewer

**At-a-glance table**

| Class | Typical use | Minimum age in handbook |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Tractor-trailer combinations | 19 |
| 2 | Large buses / school buses | 19 (21 for school bus) |
| 3 | Heavy trucks (>14,000 kg) | 19 |
| 4 | Taxi / ambulance / small bus | 19 |
| 5 | Regular passenger vehicles | 17 (after GDL completion) |
| 6 | Motorcycles | 17 |
| 7 | Instruction permit | 16 |
| 8 | Mopeds (pedal-assisted) | 17 |
| 9 | Farm tractors | 14 |

**Quick recap**
- Class tells you what you can drive.
- Beginners start with Class 7 and move toward Class 5.
- Higher-risk/commercial classes have stricter entry requirements.

## M1 CH1 Lesson 3: Your Beginner Pathway (GDL) From Day 1

### Learning outcome
By the end of this lesson, learners can describe the PEI beginner pathway and core Stage 1 restrictions.

### Page content
**Start here**  
When you receive a Class 7 instruction permit, you enter the Graduated Driver Licensing (GDL) system.

**What Stage 1 means**
- You drive only with a qualified supervising driver beside you.
- Supervising driver must have held a valid licence for at least 4 years.
- Supervising driver must not have BAC 0.05 or higher while supervising.

**Stage 1 restrictions highlighted in the handbook**
- Passenger limits apply (mainly instructor/supervisor context and immediate family conditions).
- Zero BAC rule applies to drivers in GDL.
- Beginner "L" decal must be displayed.
- No driving between `1:00 a.m.` and `5:00 a.m.` (exception noted in handbook for some Class 7 drivers age 21+).
- Demerit-point violations can trigger suspension of the permit.
- Handheld communication devices are prohibited.
- Novice Driver Course completion is required before road test eligibility.

**Road test waiting period (Stage 1)**
- With driver education: earliest first road test after `275 days`.
- Without driver education: earliest first road test after `365 days`.

**Quick recap**
- GDL is a staged safety system.
- Stage 1 is supervised, restricted, and skill-building.
- Progress depends on meeting requirements, not just time.

## M1 CH1 Lesson 4: Eligibility and Documents (What You Need Before Testing)

### Learning outcome
By the end of this lesson, learners can identify whether they are eligible to apply and what documentation they need to bring.

### Page content
**Who may apply for an instruction permit**
- Minimum age: `16`.
- Your driving privilege cannot already be under suspension.

**Primary identity documents (first permit/licence)**
- Birth certificate, or
- Passport, or
- Immigration papers

**If previous address was outside Canada**
- Canadian Immigration Identification Record of Entry form required
- Passport required

**PEI residency/address proof**
- Two pieces of acceptable residency/address information are required.
- Examples listed in the handbook include utility bills, rental/lease agreement, employment letter/pay stub, government mail, bank cheque, mortgage documents, or credit card/bank statement (with name and current PEI address).

**If applicant is under 18**
- Parent/legal guardian consent is required.
- Handbook notes consent can be withdrawn in writing, which can cancel the permit/licence.

**Common delay warning**
Most test-day delays happen because learners bring incomplete documents.

**Quick recap**
- Confirm age + legal eligibility first.
- Bring identity + residency proof.
- Under-18 applicants need parental consent documentation.

## M1 CH1 Lesson 5: The Test Journey (Knowledge, Vision, Novice Course)

### Learning outcome
By the end of this lesson, learners can explain what each pre-road-test requirement is designed to measure.

### Page content
**Knowledge test**
- Written or oral format available.
- Tests road rules, safe driving practices, and sign recognition.
- If unsuccessful, learners can rebook and retest later.

**Vision test**
- Must meet minimum standards.
- May involve color vision, distance judgment, and field-of-vision checks.
- If corrective lenses are needed, that condition is placed on the permit/licence.

**Novice Driver Course**
- Required for Class 7 holders before road test eligibility.
- Handbook lists key topics: GDL rules, demerit system, impaired driving law topics, alcohol/drug education, and handling driving conditions.
- Course is about 5 hours, usually in two sessions.

**Why these steps come before the road test**
They verify decision-making, awareness, and readiness before on-road evaluation.

**Quick recap**
- Knowledge test = rules/signs understanding.
- Vision test = visual fitness and conditions.
- Novice course = safety and risk fundamentals before road testing.

## M1 CH1 Lesson 6: Road Test Day - What Happens and How to Pass the Process

### Learning outcome
By the end of this lesson, learners can prepare correctly for the road test process and avoid preventable test cancellations.

### Page content
**Before the test starts**
- You provide the vehicle.
- You must be accompanied by a licensed driver.
- Vehicle must meet safety/equipment checks.

**Vehicle check items from handbook (examples)**
- Valid licence plates
- Lights and horn
- Rear-view mirror
- Seat belts
- Windshield/wipers/window glass
- Muffler, brakes, tires
- Valid inspection sticker
- Speedometer

**Required documents on road test day**
- Proof of insurance (pink card)
- Valid vehicle registration

**What the test is for**
The road test samples safe operation skills under typical conditions and identifies areas for improvement.

**Commonly observed skills**
- Starting/stopping
- Turning/parking
- Signs and signals
- Intersections (controlled/partly controlled/uncontrolled)
- Hazard anticipation and attitude toward other road users

**Important post-test step**
If you pass through driver education certification, the handbook states you must obtain your Class 5 photo licence within 30 days or you may be retested and pay fees.

**Quick recap**
- A safe, legal test vehicle is mandatory.
- Examiner checks practical safety behavior, not just vehicle control.
- Complete post-pass licensing steps promptly.

## M1 CH1 Lesson 7: Keeping Your Licence Valid (Renewals, Updates, Duplicates)

### Learning outcome
By the end of this lesson, learners can maintain legal licence status and avoid administrative violations.

### Page content
**Licence validity basics**
- Handbook notes driver licences expire at midnight on the driver's birthday.
- Instruction permits (Class 7) are valid for two years from issue date.

**Name and address updates**
- Name change endorsement: within 10 days (handbook wording).
- Address change report: within 30 days.
- Failing to notify is an offence under the Highway Traffic Act.

**Lost or unreadable licence**
- Apply for duplicate licence.
- Fee applies.
- Bring licence details if available, or birth certificate if details are missing.

**Responsibility when someone else drives your vehicle**
Ensure they hold a valid licence/permit and follow permit supervision rules.

**Retesting note**
The handbook allows retesting in some situations, including collisions/convictions history, suspensions, or certain medical concerns.

**Quick recap**
- Staying licensed requires ongoing admin compliance.
- Update deadlines matter.
- Keep licence status and records current.

## M1 CH1 Lesson 8: Out-of-Province and Exchange Rules (What Transfers, What Doesn't)

### Learning outcome
By the end of this lesson, learners can explain basic PEI exchange pathways and avoid assumptions about automatic transfers.

### Page content
**Driving in PEI with another licence**
- If you are 16+ with a valid licence from elsewhere, handbook says you may drive in PEI up to 120 days, subject to licence restrictions and class permissions.

**Licence exchange highlights**
- Former NS/NB residents with valid Class 5: exchange path described with no charge in handbook.
- Other Canadian provinces/territories and U.S. licences (expired less than 3 years): handbook describes exchange without written/vision/road tests, with required documentation.
- Most foreign licences: handbook says they are generally not exchangeable directly; testing pathway applies.

**Instruction permit (Class 7) exchange**
- Valid Canadian Class 7 permits may be exchangeable.
- PEI residency required.
- Additional waiting periods/course requirements may apply.
- Only Canadian instruction permits are exchangeable.

**Important learner mindset**
Do not assume transferability. Verify your exact case through current PEI channels before booking.

## M1 CH1 Lesson 9: Your Personal Licensing Action Plan

### Learning outcome
By the end of this lesson, learners can build a step-by-step plan from beginner status to compliant licensed driver.

### Page content
**Your practical action checklist**
1. Confirm your target class (most beginners: Class 7 -> Class 5).
2. Confirm eligibility (age/status) and gather ID + residency documents.
3. Complete required testing steps (knowledge, vision, novice course).
4. Follow Stage 1 restrictions consistently.
5. Book road test only when eligible by waiting period and course status.
6. Complete post-pass steps promptly (including photo licence issuance timing noted in handbook).
7. Maintain licence status through renewals and update deadlines.
8. If transferring from another jurisdiction, verify exchange pathway before making assumptions.

**Self-check before chapter quiz**
- Can I explain the difference between Class 7 and Class 5?
- Do I know the Stage 1 supervision and BAC rules?
- Do I know what documents to bring for first permit application?
- Do I know what can cancel a road test before it starts?

**Final recap for Chapter 1**
You now have the full mental map: what a licence means, which class fits your goal, how GDL works, what testing requires, and how to remain legally compliant after licensing.
